A Pen in the Style of Ross Lovegrove

Though not quite interaction design, I thought I'd share some work from my past life as an industrial designer.

Summary

The challenge: Choose a designer, and design a pen in his or her style

What I did: Sketch, prototype in clay, construct functional model

When: 2008

The result:




This is my final model. I choose this shape, because I thought it was very organic and fluid, two of Lovegrove’s key traits. I choose the indentation design, in consideration of the ergonomics of the human hand; the indentations should follow someone’s finger placement on a pen. I designed it to fit with my hand while writing, but most people who tried it had different finger placements along the indentations, so more user testing and redesign would be the next step of the process to make this a more ergonomic pen.
